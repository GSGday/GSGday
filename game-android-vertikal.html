Kenapa Game Android Vertikal Sudah Ditinggalkan?

Azhar Muhazzab 11 Juli 2025 8 menit baca

Di masa awal kejayaan Android, game-game berorientasi vertikal (portrait) begitu mendominasi. Dari Flappy Bird, Stack, hingga 2048, semua dirancang untuk dimainkan dengan satu tangan—sambil naik angkot, antri beli bakso, atau bahkan ngintip chat mantan.

Namun kini, kita hidup di era di mana game Android justru berlomba tampil horizontal (landscape). Kenapa bisa begitu? Inilah penjelasannya.

1. Evolusi Gamer: Dari Santai ke Serius

Dulu, bermain game di HP adalah soal mengisi waktu. Sekarang, game mobile punya cerita panjang, grafis AAA, dan komunitas global. Format horizontal memungkinkan:

Kontrol yang lebih kompleks (misal tombol gerak & serang)

Pengalaman visual lebih luas dan sinematik

Tampilan UI seperti game konsol

Game-game seperti PUBG Mobile, Genshin Impact, atau Honkai Star Rail tidak mungkin nyaman dalam mode vertikal.

2. Developer Ingin Game-nya Terlihat "Next Level"

Saat sebuah game tampil horizontal, secara psikologis ia terasa lebih "serius" dan "premium". Apalagi:

  • Lebih cocok untuk video trailer atau iklan YouTube
  • Mudah diintegrasikan ke kontroler fisik
  • Memberi kesan profesional dan siap bersaing dengan game PC

Hasilnya? Game horisontal makin banyak dan makin mahal.

3. Landscape Cocok untuk Monetisasi & Ekspor

Game dengan orientasi landscape lebih mudah diekspor ke:

Konsol
Porting ke platform lain lebih mudah
eSports
Turnamen membutuhkan format landscape
Cloud
Kompatibel dengan layanan cloud gaming

Ini alasan kenapa banyak game casual juga kini mulai "dipaksa" tampil horizontal.

4. Apakah Game Vertikal Sudah Mati?

Tidak juga. Game vertikal masih hidup di:

  • Genre idle, clicker, atau hyper-casual
  • Game puzzle ringan seperti Two Dots atau Moncage
  • Visual novel atau story game eksperimental (seperti Florence)

Tapi sayangnya, banyak dari game ini kini dibanjiri iklan agresif atau dianggap sebagai game "kelas B".

5. Kapan Game Vertikal Akan Bangkit?

Saat ada inovasi. Mungkin lewat:

Format short-video ala TikTok

Game interaktif berbasis AI

Cerita pendek yang bisa dimainkan di kereta atau WC

Vertikal akan kembali... tapi bukan untuk jadi raja, melainkan jadi alternatif yang segar.

Kesimpulan

Game Android kini telah berevolusi dari sekadar iseng menjadi ladang eSports dan storytelling kelas dunia.

Vertikal itu nostalgia. Horizontal itu dominasi.

In the early glory days of Android, vertically oriented (portrait) games dominated. From Flappy Bird, Stack, to 2048, all were designed to be played with one hand—while riding public transport, queuing for meatballs, or even checking an ex's chat.

But now, we live in an era where Android games compete to appear horizontal (landscape). Why is that? Here's the explanation.

1. Gamer Evolution: From Casual to Serious

In the past, playing games on mobile phones was about killing time. Now, mobile games have long stories, AAA graphics, and global communities. The horizontal format enables:

More complex controls (e.g., movement & attack buttons)

Wider and more cinematic visual experience

Console-like UI display

Games like PUBG Mobile, Genshin Impact, or Honkai Star Rail wouldn't be comfortable in vertical mode.

2. Developers Want Their Games to Look "Next Level"

When a game appears horizontally, psychologically it feels more "serious" and "premium". Especially because:

  • More suitable for video trailers or YouTube ads
  • Easy to integrate with physical controllers
  • Gives a professional impression ready to compete with PC games

The result? More horizontal games at higher prices.

3. Landscape Suitable for Monetization & Export

Landscape-oriented games are easier to export to:

Consoles
Easier porting to other platforms
eSports
Tournaments require landscape format
Cloud
Compatible with cloud gaming services

This is why many casual games are now being "forced" to appear horizontally.

4. Are Vertical Games Dead?

Not really. Vertical games still live in:

  • Idle, clicker, or hyper-casual genres
  • Light puzzle games like Two Dots or Moncage
  • Visual novels or experimental story games (like Florence)

But unfortunately, many of these games are now flooded with aggressive ads or considered "B-class" games.

5. When Will Vertical Games Rise Again?

When innovation comes. Possibly through:

TikTok-like short-video formats

AI-based interactive games

Short stories playable on trains or in restrooms

Vertical will return... but not as king, rather as a fresh alternative.

Conclusion

Android games have evolved from mere pastimes to fields of eSports and world-class storytelling.

Vertical is nostalgia. Horizontal is dominance.

- GSGday Analysis Team

And between the two, we as gamers still have choices—it's just a matter of which thumb is ready to act.
